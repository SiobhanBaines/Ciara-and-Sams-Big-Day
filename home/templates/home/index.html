York

The beautiful city of York is steeped in history and started life as a small roman military fort. The ancient Roman roads of Via Praetoria and Via Prinicipalis ran under todays streets,Stongaete and Petergate.

During the reign of the Northubrian kings, York boomed and became a centre of trade with the rest of Europe. In the 7th century Paulinius of York erected a small wooden church which later became the iconic cathedral. Over the years York has been invaded by Danes, Viking and Norman forces.The vikings gave York it's viking name of Jorvik.

It took over 250 years to build York Minster between the years of 1220 and 1475. The city if full of ginnels and snickets, cobbled alleyways and stone streets including the infamous Shambles.

York if full of places to visit including York Minster, Clifford Tower, York Castle Museum, Jorvik Viking Centre, The Shambles, National Railway Museum or walk the Medieval City Walls.
